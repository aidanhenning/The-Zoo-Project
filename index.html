Identifying Zoo Animals

The purpose of this web page is to help the incoming zookeeper interns identify each animal and understand their needs and behaviors.

Bears

  1. Ollie

    2. Weighing in at 1,200lbs and 10ft tall, this polar beast strikes fear into all who oppose him. Key identifiers include a scar behind his right ear and a birthmark on his back left leg.

    Polar Bear Facts

  2. Mona

    3. Mono is the love bug of the bunch. At 200lbs, she's the smallest of the pack. She's been known to act like a mother to the other bears, taking care of the newborns.

    Black Bear Facts

Giraffes

  1. Frankie

    2. Frankie likes to talk, and often with his mouth full. Some of Frankies hobbies include eating, cooking, and grocery shopping. Key identifiers include his insatiable hunger and large belly.

    Giraffe Facts

  2. Coconut

    3. Coconut is not the brightest but she does her best. She is very playful and loves her red bouncy ball. Key identifiers include sideways walking and coconut shaped eye patch.

    Giraffe Facts

Lions

  1. Mella

    2. Mella thinks shes a verocious beast, but she is only 5 months old and still needs snuggles from the caretakers. One day she'll be at the top. Key identifiers include her small stature and high pitched roars.

    Lion Facts

  2. Karl

    3. Karl just discovered mirrors. And Karl is obsessed with himself. Don't ever try to back Karl away from his mirror. Lions don't like their pride being tested.

    Lion Facts

Monkeys

  1. Cookie

    2. Cookie does in fact like cookies. But only chocolate chip. Don't be bringing any oatmeal rasin cookies in her habitat. She'll throw them back at you.

    Monkey Facts

  2. Earl

    3. We all know orange cat theory. Apply that same logic to monkeys and you get Earl. You will never predict his next move.

    Monkey Facts

  3. Banana Pudding

    4. Banana Pudding is the oldest living monkey on earth. Banana will stay in her bed all day and night and requires extra attention. She must be protected at all costs.

    Monkey Facts

Alligators

  1. Wren

    2. Wren is a bully. He will whip his tail in the face of other alligators for no reason. He is an instigator. Karma is coming.

    Alligator Facts

  2. Aspen

    3. Aspen has been dealing with Wren since the zoo opened. Aspen goes by another name... Karma

    Alligator Facts

  3. Mika

    4. Mika stays out of the fuss and keeps secluded on her island in the far corner. Mika is above it all. Key identifiers include a missing top left canine and brown patches on her knees.
